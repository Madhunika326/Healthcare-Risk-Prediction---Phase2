How It Works

A step-by-step breakdown of our healthcare risk prediction pipeline

1
Enter Health Information

You begin by filling out our comprehensive assessment form with 12 key health indicators:

Personal Data: Age, Gender, Residence Health Awareness: Awareness Score (1-5), Education Level Symptoms & Distance: Severity (1-5), Healthcare Distance Behavioral: Cost Fear, Hospital Fear, Care Delay Socioeconomic: Income Level, Insurance Status
2
Data Validation

Your input is validated against strict rules to ensure accuracy and consistency.

✓ Range validation (age: 18-60, distance: 1-49 km) ✓ Category validation (gender, education, insurance) ✓ Numerical constraints (0-1 scales, 0-119 day delays) ✓ Data integrity checks
3
Feature Preprocessing

Data is normalized, scaled, and encoded using our trained preprocessor for consistent model input.

→ Numerical features scaled to [0, 1] range → Categorical variables one-hot encoded → Missing values handled (if any) → Feature order standardized
4
Autoencoder Feature Extraction

Our trained autoencoder compresses your data into powerful latent representations that capture hidden patterns.

→ Encoder: 12 features → 8 latent dimensions → Learns unsupervised health patterns → Discovers non-linear relationships → Produces 8 dimensional latent vector
5
Hybrid Feature Creation

Original features are combined with latent features to create a hybrid feature set for improved predictions.

→ Original 12 features: explicit health indicators → Latent 8 features: learned health patterns → Combined 19 features: rich health representation → Maximizes prediction accuracy
6
ANN Risk Prediction

Our trained artificial neural network processes the hybrid features to generate your personalized health risk score.

→ Neural network input: 19 hybrid features → Hidden layers: 64 → 32 → 16 neurons → Activation: ReLU with Dropout regularization → Output: 0-100 continuous risk score
7
Risk Score & Recommendations

Receive your comprehensive health risk assessment with categorization and personalized health recommendations.

Low Risk (< 35): Continue current practices, regular check-ups Medium Risk (35-65): Monitor health, implement preventive measures High Risk (> 65): Consult healthcare provider, professional evaluation → Export results as PDF for your records
